# Erratt — Campaign World Bible (City of Exiles)

> Reference for the **City of Exiles** D&D 5e campaign. The party begins in
> **Utsa Paradisa** ("the City of Exiles"), a desert oasis city in the Northern
> Territories where water is a precious, sacred, heavily guarded commodity.
>
> The world was shaped by **the Schism** — an event in which the Feywild
> attempted to merge with the material plane, spilling out magic and Fey
> denizens, splitting the land, and creating the Beastfolk. Its aftermath
> defines the borders, grudges, and alliances of every nation below.

---

## The Three Great Nations

### Trivan
A large cosmopolitan area once ruled by Humans, who were overthrown
generations ago. Humans still find a home here, but the sprawling city is now a
conjoined meeting place between the nations of **Drathomir**, the **Iron Holds**,
and **Medui-Ghislerin** (the Tree of Five Families). Trivan is the hub of trade
between nations and lies at the bottom of the Schism. To the East lies the wall
of the Schism; the Azureflow River runs alongside it.

Trivanis are generally very gregarious, or good at faking it. The city is
crowded and metropolitan, and navigating its social niceties is paramount to
survival.

- **Proficiencies:** Insight, plus Persuasion *or* Deception. One bonus basic language of choice.
- **Feat (choose one):** Actor, Linguist, Observant, or Skilled.

### The Iron Holds
A sprawling mountain range presided over by the Dwarven King **Barnabus
Dumavil**. Native home to most dwarves (including Duergar), orcs, and deep
gnomes. When the Schism split the great mountain, dwarves and orcs warred over
the hollowed-out mountain for centuries until Barnabus and **Davyak Grimscythe**
signed the **Iron Grey Concordat**, cementing unity. The deep gnomes — who
suffered the war from both fronts — helped broker it.

Five Holds in total: **The Iron Hold, Tusk Hold, Dark Hold, Moon Hold, and The
Aerie**. Plus **Wharf Town**, where passing ships dock.

- **Proficiencies:** History, Survival. Bonus language: Dwarven, Gnomish, or Orcish.
- **Feat (choose one):** Durable, Inspiring Leader, or Resilient.

### Drathomir
Least impacted by the Schism — except half its territory is now **Deadwood**, a
once-lush evergreen forest devastated by the event. Adapting, the people carved
out land in the Northern Territories, leading to war with the **Goliaths**.
Drathomir claims survival; the Goliaths call them conquerors. The war stalled
when the **Dragonborn** conquered **UkVaruun**. The tenuous peace was brokered by
**Empress Selene of Utsa Paradisa**. Old grudges remain and the species still
abhor each other.

Settlements: humans bloom in **Cliffs Edge**; gnomes live among the Dragonborn;
imprisoned Goliaths are watched over in **UkVaruun**; Dragonborn populate
**Bastion** (which withstood the Goliaths' last assault); and **Blooms End**, a
small town where condemned scholars research how to revive the Deadwood.

- **Base:** Arcana proficiency. Language: Draconic or Gnomish.
- **City of Drathomir:** + Persuasion; feat — Keen Mind or Observant.
- **UkVaruun:** + Athletics, Resilient feat; may learn Giant instead of Draconic/Gnomish.
- **Blooms End:** + Nature; chance at the Shadow Touched feat.
- **Bastion / Cliffs Edge:** + skill proficiency of choice; Dungeon Delver feat.

### Medui-Ghislerin (The Tree of Five Families)
The Elven nation, living inside or near the **Great Tree** that survived the
Schism — miles tall, surrounded by an ocean floor. **Sea Elves** live in the
roots and control the port; **Drow** live within the Tree in the **Hallows**;
**Wood Elves** live throughout and man the bridge to the lush eastern forests;
**High Elves** live atop in towers; **Eladrin** are scattered and mingle where
they can (most other elves disdain them, as they hail from the plane that broke
the world). The nation has treaties with the **Firbolgs** and **Halflings** who
tend the Verdant forests to the East.

- **Proficiencies:** Animal Handling, Nature; plus one of Survival, Arcana, or History.
- **Feat:** Mobile, or Fey Touched (roll a d4 for the court).

### The Emerald Wilderness & The Riverlands
East of Medui-Ghislerin, shepherded by **Firbolgs** and **Halflings**. The
Emerald Wilderness holds reclusive settlements of folk who herald the old wood
spirits and helped broker peace after the Schism. Largest settlement: **Merry
Oak**.

- **Merry Oak:** Nature, Animal Handling; cast *speak with plants and animals* a number of times/day equal to proficiency bonus.

The **Riverlands** are fertile farmlands of halflings, firbolgs, humans, half-
elves, and beastfolk — a happy, communal, un-greedy people who know how to
celebrate.

- **Riverlands:** Performance, Survival; learn Halfling or Elvish; feat — Healer or Chef.

## The Northern Territories
Cities not centrally controlled by a superpower, yet powerful enough to impact
Erratt globally.

### Utsa Paradisa — The City of Exiles (campaign start)
The Jewel of the Desert: a large oasis city surrounded by desert, home to those
who could not venture anywhere else. Mostly inhabited by **Tieflings** shunned
long ago by nations that no longer exist, alongside a large **Tabaxi**
population and other exiled beastfolk. Water is heavily guarded and considered
sacred for its scarcity. Ruled (by implication) in the era of **Empress
Selene**.

- **Proficiencies:** Investigation, Insight; learn Infernal or Abyssal.
- **Feat:** Healer or Resilient.

### UkRuundir
Capital of the **Goliaths**, nestled in the valley of the great mountain chain.
Rough, tough, and adaptable to harsh environments; their giant blood grants
resistances. UkRuundir partners closely with Utsa Paradisa — exporting lumber
and mammoth meat in exchange for tar and spices. The desert-to-cold-forest
traders are admired for braving the crossing.

- **Proficiencies:** Athletics, Survival; learn Giant.
- **Resistance:** Cold, Fire, or Lightning (choose or roll d3).

## Races of Erratt

- **Humans** — Once held Trivan; ousted generations ago. A large population still
  fights for freedom and titles there. Now largely nomadic and welcome anywhere.
- **Elves** — Mostly in Medui-Ghislerin:
  - *High Elves* — Reserved, see themselves above others; genuinely admire gnomes.
  - *Wild Elves* — Rougher; patrol the lands around the Tree; allied with Halflings/Firbolgs.
  - *Drow* — Funeral rites in the dark Hallows darkened their skin; suspicious of outsiders; coat themselves in ancestral ash against sunlight.
  - *Sea Elves* — Most favorable to others; blue-toned, tattooed traders and diplomats of the ports.
  - *Eladrin* — Most-travelled, trickster bards; distrusted and blamed for the Schism (they hail from the Feywild).
- **Dwarves** — Renowned crafters; mostly in the Iron Mountains. Recently signed the Iron Grey Concordat with the Orcs.
  - *Duergar* — Deep dwarves; darkvision-adept; prone to a touch of madness; rumored touched by the Winter Court during the Schism.
- **Orcs / Half-orcs** — Among the most adaptable races. Post-Concordat, expanding fast; in line to rule the Iron Holds for three generations after the Dwarven King passes. *"Orcs can get it done faster, but Dwarves can get it done better!"* Dislike being seen as brutes — as likely to beat you at intellect as athletics.
- **Deep Gnomes** — Third most populous in the Iron Holds; esteemed in arcane research and tight-corner spelunking. Content with a seat at the table; the Concordat has them waiting ~450 years to rule.
- **Dragonborn** — As ancient as the Elves; inhabit Drathomir. The Ancient Dragons have long vanished.
  - *Metallic* — Consider themselves superior; hold office; clan and lineage above all; isolationist.
  - *Chromatic* — Most populous; labor and military; gregarious and well-travelled.
  - *Gem* — Rarest; hatched from gemstone nests; connected to their ancestral Hoard and always know the way back. Undertake the **Exodus** pilgrimage at coming of age (most never return). The current ruler of Drathomir is a Gem.
- **Gnomes** — Naturally adept at magic; partnered with Drathomir, advising nobility and helping Dragonborn harness their elemental gifts.
- **Goliaths** — Hardy descendants of Ancient Giants who once ruled Erratt. The Schism ended their dominance. Valiant warriors of the untamed North; tenuous relations with the Dragonborn; still long for war to retake UkVaruun.
- **Half-Elves** — Always proving themselves in Medui-Ghislerin (often as house staff); many left East to the Riverlands, where they live free of prejudice.
- **Halflings** — Mostly farm the Riverlands above the Schism. Partnered with Trivan to dig a tunnel through the Schism to Riverbend; dangerous, thankless work. Pay a tithe to the Elves.
- **Firbolgs** — Rare outside the Emerald Wilderness; gentle giants, keepers of nature who commune with ancient spirits. Often magically disguise themselves in settlements.
- **Tieflings** — Predominantly in the Northern Territories; skin hues vary by lineage and climate (desert crimson-to-yellow; mountain blue/purple/black; orc-blooded green/grey). Many were exiled before the Schism and settled in Utsa Paradisa. Gradually more accepted, still face prejudice.
- **Genasi** — Born of mortals and Djinn; lay low in cosmopolitan areas. Hunted for their blood (rumored to grant wishes); many work as fortune-tellers and bards.
- **Aasimar** — Angel-blooded; often unaware until puberty. Tell-tale silver (or rarer golden) eyes. Often asked to hide their gifts; live lonely, solitary lives.
- **Tritons** — Mysterious; deal mainly with the Sea Elves and trade with the Na Mokupuni Noe Hina. Trade pressure-hardy fruits and vegetables.
- **Tortles** — Descendants of Dragon Turtles; easygoing, welcoming islanders (though many are too scared to visit).
- **Beastfolk** — Created when the Schism's spilled magic awakened simple forest animals (cats, rabbits, lions, birds, apes) into sentient bipeds. No one knows who is Feywild-born vs. newly emerged. Ostracized and mocked, but carving out an existence.

## Pantheon

The human deities of Erratt have a **duality** depending on whether you have
honored them.

- **The Shepherd / The Chariot** — Death and transition.
  - *The Shepherd* — A humble grey-skinned, rock-faced humanoid in a rice-farmer's outfit, straw hat, and crooked crook. Welcomes you with open arms to a pleasant afterlife. Favored by farmers and travelers.
  - *The Chariot* — A grim, black-hooded figure with a chain necklace of decayed hands, whipping an endless zombie horde with a bladed chain. Come for at death, you join an endless journey gathering others for his chariot.
- **The Witness / The Herald** — Secrets and promises.
  - *The Witness* — A dark-skinned figure with no facial eyes who always knows where you are (grows eyes anywhere on his body). Grants visions and secrets — at the price of never sharing them.
  - *The Herald* — Appears when you break a promise, making your secrets known; those you sought visions about learn you broke faith.

### Race-Specific Deities
- **Dragonborn — The Wyrm of Breath:** Believed to have exhaled life onto Erratt. Symbol: a Great Winged Wyrm of Stars in a constellation, above a circle of stars (Erratt).
- **Elves — The Great Tree:** Most elves seek counsel with the Tree rather than gods, reading omens; bad omens may blacken a dead leaf to crumbling in one's hand.
- **Dwarves — The Tower / The Hammer:** A dwarven figure of rock and flame forever building a tower to the heavens — a call to constant improvement. A broken-tower omen means redeem yourself and build again, or be crushed by the Hammer.
- **Orcs — The Wind Inside:** Swirling lines etched on orc bodies; knowing when to be calm and when to be its opposite, and when to switch. Orcs temper aggression with meditation and study — the most adaptable race because they know when to catch their breath.
