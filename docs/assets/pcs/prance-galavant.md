## **Prance Galavant: Exiled Fey Lord and Reluctant Hero**

#### **Appearance**

Prance is a small Harengon with an unmistakable flair. He cuts a dramatic figure in a custom-fitted set of lacquered samurai armor, deep violet and azure, decorated with swirling fey motifs and silver filigree. The pauldrons resemble stylized lotus petals, and the tassets hang over his powerful haunches a nod to his heritage and restless agility. His fluffy tail pokes imperiously from the armor’s rear. He never dons a helmet; his large, expressive ears stand tall, twitching for effect or emphasis to any crowd's delight. His face is boldly marked with patterns of blue and silver dye—an echo of his fey lineage and a defiant reminder to all of his true nature.

He wields a slender longsword styled as a katana, its hilt wound in indigo silk. The blade is etched with Sylvan runes, visible only in silvery moonlight. When Prance takes the field, he draws the weapon with a flourishing spin, saluting both foe and potential admirers. He moves with confident, practiced grace each step, hop, or leap done as if for an invisible audience.

#### **Backstory**

Once a radiant lordling in the Feywild, Prance embodied the intoxicating positivity of their Courts. Entrusted with secrets and magics, his wit and charisma were celebrated. But hubris proved his undoing. When presented with a relic of great power—a dreamspun flute that could call forth moonbeams—Prance believed himself clever enough to bend its magic beyond the limits imposed by Fey Law. Instead, he accidentally plunged a beloved Fey gathering into turmoil, unleashing a cacophony that nearly unraveled a sacred Accord of spring and moon.

Shamed, yet unable to admit true fault, Prance tried to laugh off his error with grand gestures and spectacular apologies never quite saying "sorry." Viewing humility as beneath his grandeur, he instead swore to reclaim his glory with deeds so marvelous that the error would become legend, not shame.

The Fey Courts would hear none of it. His punishment: banishment to the Material Plane, stripped of immortality and power, transformed into a form both comical and humbling a Harengon, small among mortals, yet marked by lingering fey grace.

He landed in mortal lands, still flamboyant, still believing himself the center of every tale, compelled to make each deed thunderous and unforgettable. Prance adopted the martial discipline of wandering samurai, believing its code echoing fey ideals of glory, drama, and beautiful swordplay. Wherever crowds gather a tavern, festival, or battlefield Prance makes his entrance. Victories are not simply won; they are performed.

#### **Personality**

* Flamboyant & Proud: Grand gestures, booming pronouncements, flamboyant flair, and elegant flourishes in battle. He sees crowds as witnesses to his path to redemption, and the fey may yet be secretly watching.  
* Haunted by Shame: Beneath the bravado is fear what if the story does not end well, and he is never forgiven?  
* Glory-Driven: Every quest, duel, or rescue becomes a legend-in-the-making for him.  
* Unquenchable Curiosity: He cannot resist a mystery, relic, or opportunity for heroics.  
* Never Hides His Face: The thought of anonymity or shame is anathema.

#### **Ideals**

* Redemption Through Heroic Deeds: Heroic acts can atone for past folly.  
* Glory Inspires Others: One’s legend can lift all, not just oneself.  
* Curiosity: Wonders and dangers are meant to be uncovered.

#### **Bonds**

* The Feywild: Prance aches to return, not as a fool but as a hero whose saga is admired.  
* Exile’s Keepsake: A wooden flute, which plays only in the moonlight, a symbol of his past and hope for redemption.

#### **Flaws**

* Hubris: His pride may push him into reckless challenges.  
* Haunted by the Past: Encounters with reminders of his banishment or its victims make him reckless or introspective.  
* Showboat: He escalates any task to something grand, sometimes to the detriment of practicality or safety.

# Exile

During a sacred gathering, Prance used the dreamspun flute to amplify the ceremony. The music did not merely summon moonbeams, it opened a path between realms. An outsider used that opening to step into the Fey Court and seize power.

He was approached beforehand by a mysterious “ally” who claimed the old court had grown stagnant and false. Prance, eager to make history, agreed to help stage a dramatic magical “revelation” during the ceremony. He did not realize this would allow a usurper to invade and overthrow the court.

## **Prance’s version**

“I opened a door for beauty. Regrettably, tyranny walked through it first.”
