# Prance Galavant
*(pronounced Prawnse)*

**Player:** Gary · **Ancestry:** Harengon · **Class:** Paladin

A theatrical rabbit-knight who narrates his own legend as he lives it — "the
greatest of heroes is here to improve the situation greatly."

## Overview

Prance was once a radiant lordling of the Feywild, celebrated for wit and
charisma — until hubris undid him. Entrusted with a dreamspun flute that could
call forth moonbeams, he tried to bend its magic past Fey Law, and the door he
opened for beauty let tyranny walk through first: an outsider used the breach to
seize the court. Too proud to say *sorry*, he swore instead to win back his glory
through deeds so marvelous the error would become legend. The Courts banished him
to the Material Plane, stripped of immortality and reshaped into a small,
comical Harengon still marked with lingering fey grace.

Now he fights as a paladin — two-handed longsword and split mail, healing allies
with *lay on hands*, leaning on his rabbit traits for lucky footwork on failed
saves and a knack for brutal critical hits — dressed in lacquered violet-and-azure
samurai armor, face dyed in blue and silver, never wearing a helmet lest the crowd
miss his face. He has taken up the wandering samurai's discipline as an echo of fey
ideals: glory, drama, beautiful swordplay. Every fight is the next act of a saga,
and beneath the bravado runs a real fear — that the story will not end well, and
he will never be forgiven. Where others loot, Prance postures.

## In Character

**Ideals** — Redemption through heroic deeds; one hero's legend can lift everyone;
wonders and dangers are meant to be uncovered.

**Bonds** — He aches to return to the Feywild, not as a fool but as a hero whose
saga is admired. He carries an exile's keepsake: a wooden flute that plays only in
moonlight.

**Flaws** — Hubris that goads him into reckless challenges; a compulsion to escalate
any task into something grand, practicality be damned; reminders of his banishment
leave him rattled and brooding.

> "Bear witness, for legend sharpened its blade today!"

> "Dull swords tell dull tales — let mine carve a tale worth telling."

> "I opened a door for beauty. Regrettably, tyranny walked through it first."

## The Tidewoven Amulet

When [Naivara Tidewoven](../npcs/naivara-tidewoven.md) was carried off, she draped a
teardrop amulet over Prance's ear as a deliberate clue. The fey being inside
chose him — "You will do. You must save my daughter." Against
[Adarius](adarius.md)'s strong objections, Prance donned the amulet and swore an
oath to rescue the priestess, feeling "a spark of magic click" as he put it on.
